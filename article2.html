Life Lessons: Living Abroad

At 21, I graduated from University and felt like I needed a well deserved break from seeing, reading, solving another math problem. Eager to get out of England, I started searching on reasonable options to get out of the country with little savings. Therefore I needed to find a job- hopefully average pay with more travel opportunities. BINGO! I signed a contract as an English teacher in Shanghai.

My gap yah turned into living in Shanghai for nearly two and a half years and it has been the most eye opening experience that I hadn’t even imagined. I wouldn’t say “I found myself”, just yet, but I have enhanced some of my skills.

Inner Strength

It is mentally hard, especially the first 3 to 6 months. You are still finding your feet with a new job, finding friends and navigating the city. At the beggining of my journey abroad, I was frustrated with being constantly lost; on the wrong metro line, at the wrong address and in unnecessarily looong taxi journeys.

There is a lot you need to adapt quickly to, it is hard to adjust to a different routine and way of life, especially with a new language and a culture very different to your norm.

Confidence + Self-awareness

You become open to trying new opportunities that you probably would have never considered back at home. I joined the twerk dance group in Shanghai which was completely out of my comfort zone. Through this I am more body confident, physically fit and founded a great community.

Being abroad you start learning more about yourself and the type of person you are aspire to be. As you surround yourself with like-minded people, there is less fear of judgement and more potential to be your true self.

Empathy

Through the recent Omicron response, I am witnessing how cruel and inhumane the Chinese government are as they prioritise control and power over the peoples welfare.

This experience deepened my understanding of a culture and reality that I had very little knowledge about. It seems obvious but always worth re-stating-The negative coverage about a country does not define the entire population people of that country.

I am yet to experience how these new retained skills will change my life when I go back to my home country. I can assume that I will see my home in a new light.. for good or bad?
